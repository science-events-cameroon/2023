WoCC'23 - Women in Computer Science Cameroon

12 - 15 Dec Yaounde, CAMEROON
Colocated with Science Events

  • WoCC - Women in Computer Science Days Cameroon


    • WoCC is a seminar that seeks to show students of Cameroonian universities what women accomplish in CS worldwide. During two days, women researchers from prestigious universities around the world present their work, offer tutorials on computing tools, and give advice during a women in CS workshop. WoCC will be co-located with the CS Week (December 18 to December 22, 2023) organized by some colleagues from France.

Marie-Thérèse Abena Ondoa

Marie-Thérèse Abena Ondoa

Minister of MINPROFF
Yaounde, Cameroon

Marie-Thérèse Abena Ondoa is a pediatrician, professor and Cameroonian politician. She was appointed Minister for the Promotion of Women and the Family in 2009.

Before becoming a minister, she worked in universities and hospitals. Particularly, she is:

  • Professor at the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Yaoundé I;
  • Vice-Dean of this faculty, in charge of research and cooperation;
  • Head of the Pediatrics Department of the University Hospital Center (CHU) of Yaoundé.
Appointed director of the Yaoundé central hospital on March 17, 2009, she became the first Cameroonian woman to hold this position.

Mrs. Libom Li Likeng

Yaounde, Cameroon

Minette Libom Li Likeng is a Cameroonian politician. She has held the position of Minister of Posts and Telecommunications since October 2, 2015.

Prof Oana Balmau

Prof Oana Balmau

Assistant Professor
McGill University, Montreal, Canada

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Computer Science at McGill University, where I lead the Data-Intensive Storage and Computer Systems Laboratory (DISCS Lab). My current focus is on storage and persistent memory technologies, with an emphasis on the way we manage large-scale data for machine learning, data science and edge computing workloads. I also hold a status-only appointment at the University of Toronto.

I completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Sydney in 2020, advised by Prof. Willy Zwaenepoel. My dissertation research was on the design and implementation of efficient key-value stores for future hardware and performance requirements. My PhD was generously supported by The University of Sydney Faculty of Engineering and IT Dean's Postgraduate Research Scholarship and by the EPFL Fellowship for Doctoral Studies. I have earned my Bachelors and Masters degrees in Computer Science from EPFL.

Prof Natacha Crooks

Prof Natacha Crooks

Assistant Professor
UC Berkeley, California, USA

I am an Assistant Professor at UC Berkeley in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS). I work at the intersection of distributed systems, databases and security. I am a founding member of the SkyLab, a member of the Data Systems and Foundations Group as well as the Berkeley Center for Decentralized Intelligence.

On the industrial side, I was a Scientific Advisor at Improbable on their metaverse project and a Strategic Advisor at Astronomer. Previously, I worked at Materialize, a new streaming SQL materialized view engine, where I remain a technical advisor.
